# Sun et al. 2020 : Flexural buckling behaviour of high-chromium stainless steel welded I-section columns

## Metadata

* **Date read:** 18/06/2026

## 1. Summary: what was tested

This study investigated the minor-axis flexural buckling behaviour and resistance of welded I-section columns made of a newly developed high-chromium austenitic stainless steel (Grade EN 1.4420). The experimental programme consisted of 10 pin-ended column tests over varying lengths, using two non-slender cross-section profiles (I-150×100×6 and I-150×120×6). The headline aim was to examine the applicability of current design standards (Eurocode 3, AISC, ASCE, and AS/NZS) to the new high-strength, highly corrosion-resistant EN 1.4420 alloy.

## 2. Material properties

* **Experimental:** The authors conducted longitudinal tensile coupon tests.
* **Reporting:** The material properties ($E$, $\sigma_{0.2}$, $\sigma_{1.0}$, $\sigma_u$, $n$, and $n^\prime$) were averaged from two coupons cut from the exact same batch of virgin plates used to fabricate the column specimens (Table 2).
* **Selection Constraint:** Unlike cold-formed sections where compressive stub tests are preferred to capture corner enhancements, these are welded sections. The virgin plate tensile properties are the correct baseline to use because the fabrication impact is driven by thermal residual stresses (which the authors measured separately via the sectioning method) rather than cold-working.

## 4. Batch structure and study_id groupings

The authors specify that the tensile coupons were "cut from the same batch of virgin plates that were used for fabricating the column specimens" for all cross-sections. Because all 10 columns share the exact same set of average material properties reported in Table 2, they constitute a single material batch.

* `sun2020_A`: Welded I-sections, Grade EN 1.4420 (All 10 specimens)

## 5. Geometry and test setup

* **Geometry:** Measured raw dimensions (member length $L$, flange width $b_f$, outer section height $h$, and wall thickness $t$) are fully tabulated in Table 4. The thickness $t$ is uniform for both the web and the flanges. For the dataset, $b_f$ maps to $b$ and $h$ maps to $h$.
* **Boundary conditions:** Pin-ended (pin-pin). The columns were tested using knife-edge devices at both ends to allow rotation about the minor axis while restraining translation.
* **Effective length ($L_e$):** The authors clearly state that the distance from the specimen end to the rotation centre of the knife-edge device was 55 mm. Therefore, the effective length is reported and calculated as $L_e = L + 110$ mm (Table 4).
* **Buckling Axis:** All specimens were explicitly setup and observed to fail by flexural buckling about the minor principal axis.

## 6. Initial geometric imperfections

* **Reporting:** The initial global geometric imperfection ($\omega_g$) was physically measured using an LVDT and a CNC router prior to testing (Table 4). During the test, the actual initial loading eccentricity ($e_0$) was calculated based on strain gauge readings. The sum of these ($\omega_g + e_0$) is reported in Table 5.
* **Mapping:**
* `w_0` maps perfectly to $\omega_g$ (Table 4).
* `w_total` maps perfectly to $\omega_g + e_0$ (Table 5).
* `w_e` can be derived mathematically as `w_total` - `w_0` ($e_0$).



## 7. Failure modes and exclusions

* `n_specimens` (total in paper): 10 (Experimental) + 70 (Parametric FEA)
* `n_included` (after filtering): 10
* **Exclusions and reasons:** The 10 physical experiments are all included, as the authors confirm they all "underwent noticeable global deformations and failed by member flexural buckling about the minor principal axis". The 70 FEA data points generated in the parametric study are excluded.
* **Failure Mode Note:** Since the failure mode is explicitly confirmed, `failure_mode` will be logged as `global_flexural` for all 10 rows.

## 9. Data extraction notes

* **Tables:** Material properties (Table 2). Geometry and $\omega_g$ (Table 4). Total imperfections ($\omega_g + e_0$) and ultimate test loads $N_u$ (Table 5).
* **Units:** Initial modulus $E$ and stresses are reported in N/mm² (MPa). Dimensions and imperfections are in mm. Ultimate loads are in kN.
* **Section Type:** Recorded as `I` in the `cross_section_type` column. `forming_route` must be recorded as `welded`.

## 10. Judgement calls and flags

* **Material Selection:** Used virgin plate tensile properties. This is physically correct for welded sections where the primary fabrication effect is thermal residual stress rather than mechanical cold-working.
* **Imperfection Mapping (`w_0`, `w_e`, `w_total`):** Mapped `w_0` to the geometric bow ($\omega_g$) and `w_total` to the combined imperfection/eccentricity ($\omega_g + e_0$). `w_e` is the calculated difference ($e_0$).
* **FEA Exclusion:** Explicitly excluded the 70 FEA parametric study results to maintain the integrity of your Stage 1 and Stage 2 ML models.

## 11. General notes and useful for later

* **Design Code Performance:** The authors found that Eurocode 3 (EN 1993-1-4) provided the most accurate and consistent flexural buckling resistance predictions. Interestingly, the American specification (ASCE 8-02), which relies on the tangent stiffness method to capture nonlinearity, resulted in unsafe predictions for this specific high-chromium alloy.
