# Yang et al. 2016 : Flexural buckling behavior of welded stainless steel box-section columns

## Metadata

* **Date read:** 18/06/2026

## 1. Summary: what was tested

This study investigated the flexural buckling behaviour of welded stainless steel box-section (SHS) columns fabricated from hot-rolled plates via shielded metal arc welding (SMAW). The experimental programme tested 12 pin-ended long columns across two material grades: austenitic (S304 / EN 1.4301) and duplex (S2205 / EN 1.4462). Column lengths ranged from 1500 mm to 4000 mm, covering a non-dimensional slenderness range of 0.5 to 2.0. The primary aim was to fill the gap in experimental data for welded stainless steel columns, as most previous research focused on cold-formed sections, and to assess the applicability of current design codes (EN 1993-1-4, ASCE 8-02, AS/NZS).

## 2. Material properties

* **Experimental:** Primary properties were derived from flat tensile coupon tests machined from the parent hot-rolled plates along the rolling direction. Two stub columns (S304-130 and S2205-130) were fabricated, but these were strictly cut into strips to measure residual stresses, not to derive compressive material properties.
* **Reporting:** Full primary properties ($E_0$, $\sigma_{0.2}$, $\sigma_u$, $n$) are reported as averages per material grade in Table 2. Secondary parameters ($\sigma_{1.0}$, $n^\prime$) were not reported.
* **Selection Constraint:** Since compressive stub column properties were not obtained, the tensile coupon properties (Table 2) must be used.

## 5. Geometry and test setup

* **Geometry:** Measured dimensions (outer width $B$ and thickness $t$) are fully tabulated for all specimens in Table 1. Because these are square box-sections (SHS), the dataset's $b$ and $h$ columns will both be populated with the outer width $B$.
* **Boundary conditions:** Pin-ended (pin-pin). The columns were supported by single pole hinges.
* **Effective length ($L_e$):** The authors report both the geometric column length ($L$) and the distance between the rotation centers of the hinges ($L_t$). Based on their experimental setup, $L_t = L + 340$ mm. Therefore, $L_e$ directly maps to their $L_t$ column.

## 6. Initial geometric imperfections

* **Reporting:** The authors rigorously measured both the initial global geometric bow ($\delta_0$) and the loading eccentricities at the top ($e_{0t}$) and bottom ($e_{0b}$) of the column (Table 3).
* **Mapping:**
* `w_0` maps directly to the max initial bending value $\delta_0$.
* `w_e` maps to the average loading eccentricity, calculated programmatically as $(e_{0b} + e_{0t}) / 2$.
* `w_total` maps to the overall initial geometric imperfection $e$, calculated programmatically as `w_0` + `w_e`.



## 7. Failure modes and exclusions

* `n_specimens` (total in paper): 12 (12 physical long columns + FEA). (Note: The 2 stub columns listed in Table 1 were cut into strips for residual stress measurements, not tested structurally, so they do not count towards the specimen pool).
* `n_included` (after filtering): 12
* **Exclusions and reasons:** All FEA models are strictly excluded. The 12 physical long column tests are safely included.
* **Failure Mode Note:** The authors explicitly stated, "all the specimens failed in the form of flexural buckling without visible local buckling," despite the Eurocode theoretically classifying the duplex sections as slender (Class 4). As per our established methodology, the experimental observation overrides the Eurocode classification, so `failure_mode` will be populated as `global_flexural`.

## 8. FEA details

* **Validation:** ANSYS software with BEAM 188 elements was used. The FEA model captured buckling strengths excellently, with mean $P_{exp}/P_{FEA}$ ratios of 0.97 for austenitic and 1.01 for duplex. All FEA data rows will be filtered out to prevent data leakage.

## 10. Judgement calls and flags

* **Material Selection:** Extracted properties exclusively from the flat tensile coupons (Table 2), as compressive tests were not conducted for full-section property derivation.
* **Eurocode Conservatism Override:** Assigned `global_flexural` to all 12 tests based on explicit experimental observation, directly overriding the theoretical Class 4 local buckling expectation for the duplex series.
* **Effective Length ($L_e$):** Mapped to the authors' $L_t$ column ($L + 340$ mm) to account for the physical distance between the single pole hinges.
* **Stage 2 Imperfection Mapping (`w_0`, `w_e`, `w_total`):** Mapped `w_0` to the explicit max bow $\delta_0$. Calculated the effective loading eccentricity `w_e` programmatically as $(e_{0b} + e_{0t}) / 2$, maintaining negative signs where eccentricities opposed the natural bow.

## 11. General notes and useful for later

* **Welded Residual Stresses:** Because these are welded sections, the authors mapped the residual stresses extensively. Tensile residual stresses at the corners reached up to $0.73f_y$ for the austenitic grade and $0.41f_y$ for the duplex grade. Populating `forming_route` as `welded` in the extraction table will be crucial for the Stage 1 ML classifier to distinguish these heavily pre-stressed members from cold-formed equivalents.
* **Code Performance:** The researchers concluded that EN 1993-1-4 design rules are overly conservative for these welded box-sections, suggesting that future tailored design curves (or in your case, ML models) could unlock significant extra capacity.
