# Liu & Young 2003 : Buckling of stainless steel square hollow section compression members

## Metadata

* **Date read:** 18/06/2026

## 1. Summary: what was tested

This experimental study investigated the compressive strength of cold-formed austenitic stainless steel (Type 304) Square Hollow Section (SHS) columns. The testing programme consisted of two series of cross-sections (70x70x2 and 70x70x5) tested under pure axial compression between fixed ends. A total of 12 specimens (including 4 stub columns and 8 long columns) were tested over lengths ranging from 360 mm to 3600 mm. The headline aim was to provide experimental data on fixed-ended cold-formed stainless steel columns and compare their strengths against design rules from the ASCE, Australian/New Zealand, and Eurocode 3 specifications.

## 2. Material properties

* **Experimental:** The authors extracted material properties using both longitudinal flat tensile coupons (Table 3) and compressive stub columns (Table 4).
* **Reporting:** Full primary properties ($E_0$, $\sigma_{0.2}$, $n$) are explicitly reported for both test types. Secondary parameters like the ultimate tensile strength ($\sigma_u$) were only reported for the tensile coupons.
* **Selection Constraint:** Compressive stub column properties (Table 4) must be used for data extraction. The authors demonstrate that cold-working significantly enhances the yield strength of the finished specimens, making the stub column test a more accurate reflection of the full composite compressive stiffness of the cross-section.

## 5. Geometry and test setup

* **Geometry:** Measured dimensions (width $B$, depth $D$, and thickness $t$) are fully tabulated for all specimens in Tables 1 and 2. For the dataset, $B$ maps to $b$ and $D$ maps to $h$ (both are roughly 70 mm).
* **Boundary conditions:** Fixed-ended (fixed-fixed). The specimens were welded to end plates and bolted to rigid bearings that were locked to prevent minor/major axis rotations, twist, and warping.
* **Effective length ($L_e$):** The authors report the physical column length ($L$) and explicitly state that the theoretical effective length ($l_e$) was assumed to be one-half of the column length ($L/2$) due to the fixed-ended conditions. Therefore, $L_e$ will be derived  as $L \times 0.5$.

## 6. Initial geometric imperfections

* **Reporting:** Initial overall geometric imperfections at mid-length were measured using theodolites for both the x-axis ($\delta_x$) and y-axis ($\delta_y$) and reported as ratios of the length (e.g., 1/4970) in Table 5.
* **Mapping:**
* `w_0` maps to the maximum initial bow, calculated programmatically by taking the larger of the $\delta_x/L$ and $\delta_y/L$ ratios and multiplying by the actual specimen length $L$.
* `w_e` maps to 0.0. The researchers locked the spherical bearing into a fixed state after achieving full contact at a minimal ~2 kN load, actively preventing any functional setup eccentricity.
* `w_total` equals `w_0`.



## 7. Failure modes and exclusions

* `n_specimens` (total in paper): 12 (8 long columns + 4 stub columns).
* `n_included` (after filtering): 8
* **Exclusions and reasons:** The four stub columns (S1L0360, S1L0360R, S2L0360, S2L0360R) were explicitly designed to comply with SSRC guidelines to determine purely local, cross-sectional yielding/buckling behaviour. Under the dataset rules, these 4 rows must be strictly excluded.
* **Failure Mode Note:** The authors group the observed failure modes loosely, stating they "involved local buckling, overall flexural buckling and combined local and overall flexural buckling," but do not tag the individual 8 long columns in their results tables. Consequently, the `failure_mode` field will be left null, and `inferred_failure_mode` script will classify them based on Eurocode limits.

## 9. Data extraction notes

* **Tables:** Geometries (Tables 1 & 2). Compressive material properties (Table 4). Imperfections (Table 5). Ultimate test loads $P_{Exp}$ (Tables 6 & 7).
* **Units:** Initial modulus $E_0$ is in GPa (requires multiplying by 1000 to MPa). Stresses are in MPa. Loads are in kN. Lengths are in mm.
* **Section Type:** Recorded as `SHS` in the `section_type` column.
* **Buckling Axis:** Because these are perfectly symmetric square hollow sections, the moment of inertia is equal about all lateral axes. Thus, `buckling_axis` should be uniformly set to `-`.

## 10. Judgement calls and flags

* **Material Selection Constraint:** Extracted primary material properties strictly from the compressive stub columns (Table 4) to capture the composite cold-worked section stiffness, ignoring the flat tensile coupons.
* **Failure Mode Handling:** Left `failure_mode` null since the authors did not provide specimen-specific labels; the classifier algorithm will identify `global_flexural` vs `interactive` behavior automatically.
* **Derived Lengths:** Derived $L_e$ programmatically as $L \times 0.5$ based on the fixed-fixed boundary setup.
* **Imperfection Mapping (`w_0`, `w_e`, `w_total`):** Calculated `w_0` by extracting the maximum bow ratio from Table 5 and multiplying by $L$. Assigned `w_e` = 0.0 as bearings were locked concentrically, meaning `w_total` perfectly matches `w_0`.
* **`buckling_axis` convention:** Assigned `-` due to the symmetric nature of SHS profiles.

## 11. General notes and useful for later

* **Post-Yield Performance:** The authors observed that for the stockier, highly compact cross-sections (Series S2), the stub column tests reached forces approaching the ultimate tensile strength of the material rather than failing near the 0.2% proof stress. This highlights the massive post-yield strain-hardening capacity of stainless steel, which standard code curves (and your analytic formula without the $\beta$ factor) often underpredict.
* **Code Performance:** The Australian/New Zealand design rules were found to be slightly more reliable than ASCE and Eurocode 3 for fixed-ended SHS compression members.
