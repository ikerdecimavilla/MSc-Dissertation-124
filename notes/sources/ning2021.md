# K. Ning 2021: Experimental and numerical study of hot-rolled duplex stainless steel CHS columns

- Date read (16/06/2026):

## 1. Summary: what was tested

- investigates the flexural buckling of hot-rolled duplex stainless steel CHS columns under axial compression
- 7 pin-ended long column tests across 2 cross-section sizes with non-dimensional slenderness from 0.63 to 2.18
- Primary aim was to fill gap in data for hot-rolled stainless steel CHS members and to assess accuracy of design specifications.

## 2. Material properties

- Derived from flat tensile coupoms cut directly from the reserved tubes of the same batch
- Three coupons tested per cross-section then averaged
- Full properties averages - no secondary RO parameters
- Tensile coupon properties to be used for data extraction


## 3. Ramberg-Osgood exponent n 

- n_source = measured
- Extracted from full stress-strain curves obtained during the test

## 4. Batch structure and study_id groupings

- There are 2 physical material batches corresponding to 2 nominal CHS sizes, each sharing a unique set of averaged tensile coupon test results

Chosen Batch groups:
- ning2021_A: 114x6 (Duplex 2205)
- ning2021_B: 219x6 (Duplex 2205)

Parametric FEA studies excluded for now

## 7. Failure modes and exclusions

- `n_specimens` (total in paper): 122 (7 physical tests + 115 FEA models)
- `n_included `(after filtering): 7 long columns
- **Exclusions and reasons:** 115 FEA models are excluded to prevent dataset inflation and data leakage across train/test split

## 8. FEA details

- **Validation:** mean FE-to-test ultimate load ratio was 1.00 with a COV of 0.01
- Used ANSYS with BEAM188 elements
- Residual stresses not considered in models because the membrane residual stresses in hot-rolled sections are relatively low and have little effect on column performance

## 11. General notes and useful for later

- Existing design methods (Chinese and American)were found to underestimate the flexural buckling resistance
- EC3 provided the most accurate predictions but was very unsafe at low slenderness 
- Authors propose modifying EC3 curve by changin imperfection factor to 0.42 and limiting slenderness to 0.26
